APBA-SV Applauds the Nomination of Justice Tani Cantil-Sakauye for Chief Justice of the California Supreme Court

PALO ALTO, CA – The Asian Pacific Bar Association of Silicon Valley applauds Governor Schwarzenegger's nomination of Justice Tani Cantil-Sakauye of the Court of Appeals as the next Chief Justice of the California Supreme Court. If approved by the three-member Commission on Judicial Appointments and by voters in November, Justice Cantil-Sakauye would be the first Asian American to lead the state's judiciary and give the California Supreme Court a female majority for the first time in its history.

“The nomination of Justice Cantil-Sakauye is a historic moment for the Asian Pacific American legal community. We thank Governor Schwarzenegger for nominating a person who is both extremely qualified and reflects the rich diversity of our state,” said Rudy Kim, president of the Asian Pacific Bar Association of Silicon Valley.

Justice Cantil-Sakauye is currently an Associate Justice for the Third District Court of Appeal in Sacramento. Prior to that, she served as a superior court judge for the Sacramento County Superior Court and as a municipal court judge for the Sacramento County Municipal Court. Justice Cantil-Sakauye served in the office of Governor Deukmejian and as a deputy district attorney in the Sacramento County District Attorney’s Office.

Justice Cantil-Sakauye’s life embodies the “American Dream.” She is the daughter of a former Filipino farm worker and worked to support herself throughout her higher education including law school. Justice Cantil-Sakauye has been and remains active in her community, including with her church, the National Asian Pacific American Bar Association, and the Asian/Pacific Bar Association of Sacramento.

Justice Cantil-Sakauye must be confirmed by the California Commission on Judicial Appointments and then approved by voters on Nov. 2, 2010, before she may serve as Chief Justice.
